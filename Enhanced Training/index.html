Training & Discipline

Golden Retrievers thrive with positive reinforcement and gentle guidance. Design a training cadence tailored to your puppy’s age and energy.

Positive Reinforcement

  • Reward desired behaviors immediately with treats or praise.
  • Use consistent cues across all family members.
  • Keep sessions upbeat, short (~5 minutes), and frequent.

Crate Conditioning

  1. Introduce the crate with cozy bedding and treats.
  2. Close the door briefly, building duration slowly.
  3. Use the crate for safe rest, never as punishment.

Potty Routine

Take your pup outside first thing, after meals, naps, and every 1–2 hours. Celebrate successful potty breaks to reinforce the habit.

Skill Progression

Introduce sit, down, come, and leash manners early. Enroll in puppy kindergarten around 12–16 weeks to pair structured learning with socialization.

Nutrition Blueprint

Select a large-breed puppy formula that meets AAFCO standards. Monitor body condition by feeling ribs without seeing sharp outlines, and adjust portions based on growth and activity.

  • 8–12 weeks: Four meals daily to sustain energy and balanced growth.
  • 3–6 months: Transition to three meals, measuring portions to prevent overeating.
  • 6–12 months: Two meals, maintain lean physique to protect developing joints.

Treat Smart

Use part of the daily kibble as training rewards. Keep treats under 10% of daily calories and choose puppy-safe options.

Hydration & Transition Tips

Offer fresh water throughout the day and pick up bowls an hour before bedtime to support night-time potty success. Transition to adult food gradually over a week once your pup reaches 12 months and your vet gives the green light.

Grooming Rituals

Create calm, rewarding grooming experiences to keep that signature golden coat radiant.

Brushing

Brush weekly with a slicker brush, increasing to daily during seasonal shedding to prevent mats and distribute natural oils.

Bathing

Bathe every 4–6 weeks or after muddy adventures. Always brush first, use dog-safe shampoo, and rinse thoroughly.

Paw & Nail Care

Trim nails monthly (or weekly tips) and tidy paw fur with blunt scissors for traction and cleanliness.

Ears & Teeth

Inspect ears weekly, drying after swims. Brush teeth several times a week with dog toothpaste to build lifelong dental habits.

Health Care Compass

Vet Milestones

  • 8 weeks: Schedule initial exam, parasite screening, and first vaccinations.
  • 12 & 16 weeks: Continue DHPP boosters, discuss optional vaccines.
  • 12 months: Annual boosters and health assessment.

Preventatives

Start heartworm, flea, and tick prevention per veterinary guidance. Maintain a lean physique to protect developing joints.

Exercise Wisdom

Follow the 5 minutes per month of age guideline for structured exercise, and prioritize low-impact play and swimming.
