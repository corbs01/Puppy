The Big Picture

Reward-based, micro-training sessions fuel fast learning and a lifelong bond.

Training Style

Short, reward-rich sessions (1–3 minutes) with many repetitions. Always finish on a high note to lock in confidence.

16-Week Goals

  • Reliable name response & crate love
  • Potty rhythm & gentle mouth
  • Sit, Down, Come, Leave-It essentials
  • Calm near the cat & happy alone-time
  • Easy handling for vet & grooming

Management Musts

Baby gates, tethers, an x-pen, and leashes keep practice perfect and the cat’s refuge secure.

Before You Bring Her Home

Stage the environment so success is the only option.

Setup Checklist

  • 42″ crate with divider, x-pen, and baby gates
  • 2–3 chew types: stuffable rubber, durable chew, soft chew
  • 6-ft leash, flat collar, front-clip harness
  • Food + pea-sized treats or kibble for reps
  • Puzzle feeders & snuffle mat
  • Elevate or gate cat resources with multiple escape routes
  • Scent swap cloths between cat & puppy
  • Schedule first vet visit: vaccines, microchip, preventatives, spay timing

The First 48 Hours

Gentle structure helps her land softly and fall in love with her new routines.

Quiet Landing

Create one safe “base camp” room. Let exploration expand slowly from there.

Crate Introduction

3–4 micro-sessions/day: toss 5–10 treats inside, close the door while she enjoys a stuffed toy, reopen to end on success.

Potty Plan Day 1

Outdoors every wake-up, after meals & play, and every 45–60 minutes while she’s awake.

Daily Rhythm (8–12 Weeks)

Blend training, play, and rest with predictable beats.

Morning

  • 06:30 Potty → calm greeting → breakfast puzzle
  • 07:00 3–5 min training (name, sit) → play/chew → potty
  • 09:30 Crate/x-pen nap (door closed)

Midday

  • 11:00 Potty → training (come) → controlled cat look & treat game
  • 12:30 Lunch → potty → chew/settle

Afternoon

  • 15:00 Potty → training (leave-it) → sniffy walk
  • 18:00 Dinner → potty → gentle play/enrichment

Evening

  • 20:30 Potty → settle practice
  • 22:00 Last potty → bed (expect 1–2 night breaks)

Exercise guide: ~5 minutes of structured work per month of age, up to 2–3x/day plus generous sniffing.

House-Training Blueprint

Consistent timing and celebration build habits in 2–4 weeks.

Management

Supervise or use crate/x-pen—no free roaming until successes stack up.

Timing & Spot

Take her out on wake, after meals/play, and on a 45–60 minute timer. Use the same spot and cue as she starts.

Reward & Reset

Deliver a treat + “Good potty!” within 2 seconds. Gentle interrupt accidents, head outside, clean with enzymatic solution.

Milestones

  • Week 2: Most pees outside with management
  • Week 4: Dry nights possible
  • Regression? Tighten schedule & supervision for a few days.

Training Toolkit

Micro-sessions, high-value paychecks, and playful practice.

Crate & Alone-Time

  • Goal: crate = chew buffet + naps; never a punishment.
  • Toss 5–10 treats, close door briefly, reopen. Add stuffed/frozen toys for 10–20 min.
  • Build to 30–60 min calm rest 1–2x/day.
  • Alone-time ladder: gated room → door practice → short departures → 15–30 min calm.

Bite Inhibition & Chewing

  • Provide 2–3 legal chews daily; rotate textures.
  • Redirect nips: pause play, offer chew, resume when she chooses it.
  • Persistent biting? 30–60 sec behind a gate (not the crate) to reset.

Core Cues

  • Name Game: Name → treat for eye contact; add distractions.
  • Sit/Down: Lure, name the cue after 5–10 reps, fade lure.
  • Come: Two-person ping-pong in hallway; jackpot rewards, add long line outside.
  • Leave-it: Closed fist → open palm → floor with leash.
  • Drop: Trade up, give the item back sometimes.
  • Settle: Reinforce mat contact, shape to down, feed slowly for calm.

Leash & Greetings

  • Reward at your side for one step, then two, then three—practice indoors first.
  • No jumping: pay four paws on the floor; cue sits; manage with leash/tether.

Socialization (8–16 Weeks)

  • Pair new people, surfaces, sounds, and handling with treats; keep sessions brief.
  • 1–2 new experiences/day, always ending while she’s comfortable.
  • Secure car rides with crate/harness; practice gentle body handling.

The Cat–Dog Friendship Plan 🐱+🐶

Structured phases that protect your cat and build positive associations.

Principles

Safety first, managed proximity, and constant reinforcement for calm choices.

  • Cat always has escape routes & litter privacy.
  • Dog on leash or behind a barrier until calm is default.
  • Both animals earn rewards for serene behavior.

Phase 0–2

Prep: Scent swap, feed through closed door, create cat-only zones.

Parallel Life: Swap spaces, reward noticing and checking back.

Sight-but-Separate: LAT game at gates, 1–3 min sessions, end while calm.

Phase 3–5

Shared Space: Puppy leashed with chew, cat sets pace.

Drag-Line Freedom: Short mingles with light line until 7–10 calm sessions.

Normal Life: Maintain cat-only refuges; reinforce chill near the cat.

Extra Tips

  • Train/feed the puppy before cat sessions to reduce arousal.
  • Schedule cat play when the puppy is crated with a chew.
  • Protect litter with top-entry boxes or cat-width gates.

  • Consider positive-reinforcement puppy class.

Common Hiccups & Fixes

Spot stress early and pivot to easy wins.

Increase distance, upgrade treats, reinforce settle-on-mat, and try a decompression walk before the next meeting.

Give the cat a full dog-free day, return to barrier work, and ensure vertical routes with predictable feeding.

Reinstate 45-minute potty timer and tighten supervision for 3–5 days.

Keep late-evening activity calm, potty right before bed, cover the crate, and keep overnight trips out/in low-key.

Resources to Teach Early

Hand Target

Nose-to-palm touch redirects excitement away from cats or guests.

Go to Mat

Parking cue for when life is busy—layer calm treats and praise.

Find It

Scatter food breaks fixation, adds sniffing calm, and is a fun reset.

Feeding & Health Basics

  • Split meals into 3 per day until ~12 weeks, then 2 meals.
  • Use meal portions for training whenever possible.
  • Keep fresh water stations separate for cat and dog.
  • Teething peaks at 4–5 months: increase safe chews & frozen stuffables.
  • Ask your vet about exercise limits for growing joints.
  • Follow local vaccination schedules guided by your vet.
